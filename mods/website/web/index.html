Most blockchains force users to pay for forever storage and require separate payments for off-chain data. Saito fixes both problems.

Saito applications only pay for the on-chain time they need, while the network uses a new kind of mining that secures the blockchain with fee-routing infrastructure. The result is lower fees and higher throughput than any other blockchain.

Revolutionary Technology:

Saito has patent protection on the fundamental mechanisms needed to support a scalable chain: market-driven rent for on-chain storage, and cryptographic routing signatures to identify and pay nodes that route transactions. We are committed to the launch of a public blockchain that will bring these benefits. The two key techniques are:
Transaction Rebroadcasting

Saito prunes data by default, but keeps old data circulating on-chain if they are able to out-competes new transactions for space on the blockchain. The key advance is a form of rent that does not require developers to hardcode economic variables.

Transaction broadcasting allows for the elimination of blocksize limits, as block producers have financial inentivize to limit scalability once the marginal cost of adding more data grows above the marginal revenue from doing so. Economics keep the chain from getting so big it collapses, while transaction fees fall as transactions only need to pay for forever if they need it.

Saito Consensus

Saito uses a new form of mining that pays the nodes which collect transactions from users. Cryptographic signatures and a provably-fair lottery make this as secure as mining: attackers cannot collect payments from transactions that do not include them on their cryptographically-secured routing paths.

In addition to eliminating the 51 percent attack, Saito's consensus mechanism incentivizes the provision of whatever routing infrastructure, software, and off-chain archival services are needed to drive transaction volume. No separate fees are needed to pay for scale.
